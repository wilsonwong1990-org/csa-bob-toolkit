**We are prescriptive, proactive, and prepared** as individual contributors, people leaders and cross-functional teams. We **deliver** scalable, tailored developer success engagements with measurable urgency (pace), consistency (quality) and operational agility (tempo).

## QBR/EBC Workflow

QBRs **strengthen the partnership between your business and your customer's**. They foster relationships between executives on both sides and allow you to highlight the ROI of your product.

### Prep Checklist (5-7 days)

- [ ] **Agenda:** What is the goal of this conversation
- [ ] **Evaluate:** Who will benefit from this - Identify the people who will execute
- [ ] **Customize:** How & why are we doing this
- [ ] **Reports:** Who will share & the audience
- [ ] **Attendees:** Leader, presenter, moderator, note taker roles
- [ ] **Materials:** Reports to pull, metrics to know (past year AND year ahead)
- [ ] **Format:** QBR format and tools to use

## Risk CTA Process

### Risk Types

#### External Influence
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Layoffs / Reduction in Force | Dev population decreases |
| M&A | Customer acquired by company using competitive platforms |
| Macroeconomic Conditions | Business struggles from environment/policy |
| Contracting | MSFT bundles, move to Metered, etc. |
| Technical | Outage, Incident/Bug, Platform Issues |

#### Adoption
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Failure to Launch | Initial launch activation failure or users fall off quickly |
| Technical Blockers | Security reviews, provisioning, migrations, etc. |
| IT Gatekeepers | POC's fail to move project forward or resist change mgmt |

### Risk Tracking in Gainsight

**Naming Conventions:** `[Type - Name - Reason]`

**Forecasting Risk:**
- Should always be a negative number
- Attrition: `[Seats at risk estimate] x [Annual cost per seat] = -$ARR`
- Full Churn: `Total ARR / Spend on product`

**Risk CTA Cadence:**
- RA's & Escalations: Biweekly updates via Timeline

### Best Practices for Risk Updates
- Link GitHub Issues for Product/Feature Gaps
- Summary should include:
  - What is at risk and how you discovered it
  - What's driving the decision
  - Who are the key decision makers
  - Who are our champions
  - Timeframe to mitigate
  - 2-3 bullets on overall strategy
  - Executive alignment on both sides
  - Math breakdown for forecast number

## GHAS Rollout Playbook - Key Points

### Enabling Success & Driving Adoption

#### First Steps and POC
- Understand any Pilot/POC findings and business value achieved
- Create plan for current blockers
- Ensure appropriate number of licenses purchased
- Understand how billing works with active committers

#### Planning Rollout
Work with customer to understand:
- Success criteria and GHAS goals
- High level security objectives mapped to GHAS features
- Target specific features

**Recommended Blueprint:**
1. Documentation
2. Enablement
3. Remediation

Track milestones and progress in a shared customer repo project board.

Consider the **keys to a successful organizational rollout** and share with customer. Seek regular cadence for progress updates, feedback, and iteration.
